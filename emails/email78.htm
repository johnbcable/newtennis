CLASSIFICATION OF WINES (STYLE)

The white wine style is based on a sweetness scale from 1 to 8 - 1 being bone dry through to 8 being the sweetest.  The red wine style guide is based on fullness on a scale A to E - A being the lightest and E the fullest.

 

FRENCH WHITE

BIN        STYLE

 

061       2-3            “LES PETITS GRAINS” – DOMAINE BERGON – VIN DE PAYS D’OC – 2004 – 13%

This wine is a blend of 90% Vermentino and 10% Sauvignon Blanc. Vermentino offers aromatic fruit flavours, apples and peaches, together with exquisite floral hints and gives a lovely, lingering finish. In that sense, it is similar to Viognier. Marry this with the herbaceous and gooseberry flavours of Sauvignon and you have a fine, balanced wine, wonderfully fresh and stimulating. It is ideal for drinking on its own, with white meats or salads. This wine has received many favourable comments. 3 FOR £13. Tremendous value for a single domaine wine.

 

168       2            MÂCON-VILLAGES – A.O.C. – ROPITEAU - 2003 – 13%

The very best wines of Mâcon are from individual villages; with the collective area given the title “Mâcon-Villages”. Personally I prefer the extra crispness of Mâcon to the whites from the main Burgundy region. To me they are more like Chablis then Bourgogne Blanc and to quote Hugh Johnson, “few modest wines anywhere are as reliably fresh and wholesome”. This is traditional unoaked Chardonnay at its very best. The colour is pale golden with a nose of lifted citrus notes. The palate has restrained butteriness with that delightful lemon citrus edge which distinguishes unoaked old world Chardonnay from “blousy, in yer face” over-oaked new world examples. The finish is full and very long. This is a superb wine indeed. I won’t need to convince those who prefer such styles, but for those who are tired of over-oaking masking the subtleness of the chardonnay grape, just give this wine a try. Prices for Mâcon-Villages on the High Street range from £5 to £10 depending on quality. This example retails for above £7; my price is 3 FOR £18.

 

171       2            POUILLY-FUMÉ – A.O.C. – “LA TUILERIE” - 2003 – 12.5%

The wine area of Pouilly-Fumé is situated in the upper Loire valley on the opposite bank to Sancerre. On these chalky hills, cut by the river, Sauvignon Blanc can still make better wine than anywhere else in its worldwide plantations. Its character is often described as gunflint; it is smoky (hence the suffix Fumé), slightly green, slightly spicy and has an intense appeal. The soil in Pouilly produces fruitier wines than Sancerre. This wine, which incidentally was only bottled earlier this year, is still quite young and fresh; indeed there is a certain amount of petillance in the wine when first opened. The nose is quite intense and typically Sauvignon – gooseberry, crushed blackcurrant leaves combined with vegetal notes. This is continued on the palate where there is still pleasant crisp acidity which almost puts it into a number 1 on the dryness scale; however the amount of fruit in the middle palate counter balances this perfectly. The finish is both smooth and long, very long indeed. Wines like this sell for up to £10 per bottle; my price is just 3 FOR £23.

 

ITALIAN WHITE

 

027       2            CHARDONNAY/PINOT GRIGIO – “CANTI” – I.G.T. – VENETO – 2003/4 – 12.5%

GERMAN WHITE

 

002       2-3            RIESLING – PFALTZ – QUALITÄTSWEIN – “FIRE MOUNTAIN PRIVATE RESERVE” – 2004 – 13%

Riesling is Germany’s most noble grape and is now being harvested and vinified to produce superb drier wines like this. The nose has youthful fresh fruit aromas with slightly honeyed notes. Ideal for drinking on its own, with white meats or fish, and as an aperitif. This wine will appeal equally to lovers of dry wines and to those who prefer medium sweet styles. Although it is shown on the label as a “2” in dryness, I put it as a “2-3”. My selling price is just 3 FOR £14; this for a wine that sells for over £5.

 

AUSTRALIAN WHITE

 

082       2            CHARDONNAY - McGUIGAN’S “THE BLACK LABEL” - SOUTH EASTERN AUSTRALIA – 2004 – 13.5%

This superb Chardonnay is fruit driven with peach and guava being dominant. All the wine is cold fermented using selected yeast strains and 30% was put through a secondary fermentation, converting malic acid into lactic acid generating extra complexity. Minimal oak was used in the making of this wine and acts to give subtle cinnamon/vanillin and char overtones. As the wine has great depth of flavour and distinctive varietal character, Black Label Chardonnay can be enjoyed with almost all starters and main meals, as well as with dishes such as pizzas, pastas and Asian cuisine. A wine style with universal appeal that sells for up to £6.49; my price is just 3 FOR £17.

 

 

SOUTH AFRICAN WHITE

 

157       2            DRY MUSCAT – “SEA OF SERENITY” – WINE OF ORIGIN WORCESTER – 2003 – 12%

Dry Muscat is not the oxymoron it would first appear. Certainly the Muscat grape has been associated with sweet wines but over recent years dry wines have been developed, particularly in Australia and, as with this wine, South Africa. This wine was commissioned for a chain of pub restaurants and given the brand name “Sea of Serenity” enabling me to make an awful pun by saying that I am “over the moon” with it. Wines made from Muscat grapes are often cited as the only ones that actually taste of grapes but there is more that just that to this wine. It has an intense floral nose leading to an equally concentrated palate of aromatic spice and fruit. There is a crisp, refreshing finish and the whole is absolutely gorgeous. This wine is so full of flavour, and served chilled is one of the few white wines to complement Indian and Chinese cuisine. Alternatively just try it on its own.  I urge you to try this wine. The flavour will amaze you.  You will not find wines like this in the supermarket; and certainly not at my price of only 3 FOR £12.

 

BRONZE MEDAL WINNER – 2005 INTERNATIONAL WINE CHALLENGE

099       2            SAUVIGNON BLANC – NIEL JOUBERT – WINE OF WESTERN CAPE – 2005 – 13%

I just love Sauvignon Blanc, it is probably my favourite white grape, and this a shared by many of you; I certainly sell more Sauvignon than Chardonnay. This is yet another stunning wine, showing all the grassy, flowery, gooseberry characteristics that a good Sauvignon should have. The grapes were sourced primarily from the south valley of Simonsberg and hand harvested for maximum quality. Cool fermentation has retained all the fresh fruit elements. The colour is pale straw and the nose is typically flowery. The palate is gooseberry, passion fruit and crushed blackcurrant leaves with long mouthy feel and a finish that goes on and on. Young, fresh Sauvignons such as this are simply delightful. The 2004 wine won a bronze medal at “The International Wine Challenge” this year and I have now moved to the even better 2005 vintage. I had to drink the last 2 bottles of the 2004 wine to ensure this but we all have to make sacrifices! One of the best lower priced Sauvignons I have ever tried; my price 3 FOR £15 is great value.

ARGENTINE WHITE

 

NEW VINTAGE – NEW LABEL - SILVER MEDAL WINNER – 2005 INTERNATIONAL WINE CHALLENGE

184       2            TORR0NTÉS – FINCA DE ALTURA – CALCHAQUI VALLEY – 2004 – 12.5%

Torrontés is a grape variety originating from Galicia in northern Spain, now indigenous to Argentina. It excels in the Calchaqui Valley, situated some 2200 metres above sea level in the Andes foothills and the most northerly vineyard in Argentina. The word unique is often overused, but here is the only word to describe the flavour of Torrontés. The nose is floral, almost Germanic, with grapey, Muscat aromas. The palate explodes with flavour – melon, lychee, grapefruit and fresh pineapple, with spicy and peppery notes. The finish is very, very long with a pleasant dryness. If you’re tired of ordinary Chardonnay, fed up with average Sémillon, then I implore you to try this wine. I have already converted many – why not join them? This will revolutionise your opinion of white wine – it is absolutely gorgeous. I have now moved on to the new label from this winery and this is the very latest vintage available. Very competitively priced at 3 FOR £15 for a style of wine you would not find in your supermarket.

                       

SPARKLING WINE

 

939       2            CAVA BRUT - METADO TRADICIONAL– “CASITO” – MARQUÉS DE MONISTROL – 11.5%

This superior sparkling wine is made strictly in accordance with the traditional method in the cellars of Marqués de Monistrol, using specific local grape varieties that impart the flavour that is unique to Cava amongst sparkling wines. The traditional method employed, is of course secondary fermentation in bottle, the same as for champagne, and it is this which provides the longer lasting sparkle when compared with other types of sparkling wine. Brut Cava is never as dry as brut champagne, this is just off-dry with loads of fruit along with those lovely toasty notes. The mousse is superb and very long lasting, making this a celebratory wine for all occasions, or just for drinking on its own. There will be lots of cheap Cava in the supermarkets this Christmas – but that’s just what they are – cheap wines, not worthy of the name Cava. Casito, however, is a superb Cava and great value at the now lower price of 3 FOR £14.

169       C            BOURGOGNE-ROUGE – PINOT NOIR – A.O.C. – ROPITEAU - 2002 – 12.5%

Burgundy is the traditional home of Pinot Noir and it is here that the very best wines from this capricious grape are made. These of course are the single vineyard wines which command very high prices. However with Burgundy it is the “négotiant/éleveur” that is most important and this applies equally to the more modest “Bourgogne-Rouge” such as this wine. Ropiteau Frères was established in 1848 and is a well-respected winemaker. This is typical Pinot Noir, light cherry red in colour with strawberry and raspberry on the nose and cherry and raspberry on the plate with that pleasant earthiness in the background and a good, lengthy finish. Incidentally the 2002 vintage was rated as outstanding. Supermarkets sell their basic red burgundy for around £5.50. You can now buy an infinitely better wine for roughly the same price from binendwines. This quality wine would retail for around £7; my price is 3 FOR £18.
