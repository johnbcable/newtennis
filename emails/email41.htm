CLASSIFICATION OF WINES (STYLE)

The white wine style is based on a sweetness scale from 1 to 8 - 1 being bone dry through to 8 being the sweetest.  The red wine style guide is based on fullness on a scale A to E - A being the lightest and E the fullest.

082       2            McGUIGAN – “THE BLACK LABEL CHARDONNAY” - SE AUSTRALIA – 2003 – 13.5%

A medium full bodied wine showing full buttery flavours along with tropical fruit and a touch of cinnamon. The wine is pale golden in colour and has a silky mouth feel along with fresh acidity and good length. This superb Chardonnay is fruit driven with peach and guava being dominant. The grapes were mainly sourced from The Murray Valley together with other areas and the fruit was chilled directly into a press avoiding skin contact to maintain fresh fruit aromatics. All the wine is cold fermented using selected yeast strains and 30% was put through a secondary fermentation converting malic acid into lactic acid generating extra complexity. Minimal oak was used in the making of this wine and acts to give subtle cinnamon/vanillin andchar overtones. As the wine has great depth of flavour and distinctive varietal character, Black Label Chardonnay can be enjoyed with almost all starters and main meals as well as with dishes such as pizzas, pastas and Asian cuisine. A wine style with universal appeal that sells for around £5.99 in the Independent sector.  3 FOR £16.

  

089       2-3            “WHITE CLOUD” – NOBILO WINES - NZ NORTH ISLAND – 2002 – 10.5%

The name "White Cloud" is derived from Aotearoa - Land of the Long White Cloud - the ancient Maori name for "New Zealand." It is produced by the world famous “House of Nobilo” and has been a "household" brand in New Zealand since 1990. Incidentally, for those golfers amongst you, yes it is the same family as Frank Nobilo, a notable New Zealand golfer of a few years ago. This wine is their blended style, made predominately from well-ripened Muller Thurgau and Muscat varieties from the Gisborne region. The wine is made in an off-dry to medium style. The nose is citrus lemon and lime, which follows through to the palate. There is a slight pleasant prickle on the tongue giving a fresh dryness. The middle palate shows rich, slightly sweeter fruit and culminates with a long and aromatic finish. This is one of the nicest medium priced white wines I have tasted this year. The House of Nobilo was awarded the supreme award, the Trophy, in the 2003 International Wine Challenge as “THE NEW ZEALAND WINEMAKER OF THE YEAR”.  This wine retails at around £5.49 in many outlets.  3 FOR £14 or 6 FOR £27.

 

090       2            “THE CROSSINGS” – SAUVIGNON BLANC – NZ  MARLBOROUGH – 2002 – 13%

Marlborough has long been regarded as the region for “New World” Sauvignon Blanc; some would even say the best in the whole world. Certainly, a wine such as Cloudy Bay commands a price few of us would contemplate. However, discerning wine drinkers are now distinguishing between the valleys that partly make up the Marlborough region, with wines from the Awatere Valley being regarded as of the highest quality.  Nineteenth Century settlers stopped in this valley at the point where they were able to wade their horses across the Awatere River. They called this point "The Crossings". Today grazing land has been replaced with vines, and the wine, especially the Sauvignon Blanc, coming from The Crossings three vineyards opens up new possibilities to enjoy flavours and textures that are different on the palate, even from other vineyards in the same valley. The grapes for the 2002 vintage were grown in Marlborough's Awatere Valley on the estate’s vineyards. The blend is predominantly comprised of fruit from their Brackenfield vineyard with a small percentage coming from the Medway River Vineyard. The wine was fermented using modern techniques and neutral yeasts to retain as much fruit as possible. The wines from parcels of individually harvested grapes were back-blended to give a wine representative of the whole vineyard. Bottling was then carried out after the wine had spent a couple of months on light lees and was achieved with a minimum of filtering. This 2002 Sauvignon has a pale yellow colour with green highlights. Its nose is characterised by a lovely mix of tangy lemon and herbal notes. These run into a rich palate full of ripe currants and honeysuckle, which rest on a lingering mineral base. At the time of initial release from the winery, it was described as “A wine which looks great now and will continue developing positively throughout 2003-4”. After trying it, I feel it will continue to drink well into 2005. This wine has just been selected as the only New Zealand Sauvignon available in the entire Hyatt hotel chain in the USA. It’s that good! A rarely seen wine in the UK, my price of 3 FOR £21 is some £2.50 a bottle less than its retail value.

The ever so slight off-dry character of this wine provides a delightful fruitiness and “drinkability”. Attention grabbing nose packed with fresh, ripe tropical notes with delightful mingle of citrus, guava and granadilla. Delicious in the mouth, with ever-so-lively tingle, yet ultra smooth across the palate with a clean zingy finish. Beware the 15% alcohol as it drinks just too easily! Like a good book – hard to put down! The normal retail price of this wine is about £4.49; my price is 3 FOR £13

SPARKLING WINE

 

939       2            CAVA BRUT - METADO TRADICIONAL– “CASITO” – MARQUÉS DE MONISTROL – 11.5%

This superior sparkling wine is made strictly in accordance with the traditional method in the cellars of Marqués de Monistrol, using specific local grape varieties that impart the flavour that is unique to Cava amongst sparkling wines. The traditional method employed, is of course secondary fermentation in bottle, the same as for champagne, and it is this which provides the longer lasting sparkle when compared with other types of sparkling wine. Brut Cava is never as dry as brut champagne, this is just off-dry with loads of fruit along with those lovely toasty notes. The mousse is superb and very long lasting, making this a celebratory wine for all occasions, or just for drinking on its own. I can still offer this Cava at 3 FOR £14. There will be plenty of cheap Cava around the supermarkets this festive season, but that is exactly what it is – cheap! Buy and serve quality this Christmas.

044       D         CÔTES DE CASTILLON–A.C.–CHÂTEAU LA GASPARDE–JEAN-PIERRE JANOUEIX–1998 – 13%

This is another wine with a “House of Albert Roux” label produced for his catering empire by a renowned winemaker. The Côtes de Castillon appellation is situated on the northern bank of the River Dordogne and Château La Gasparde is on the same slopes that further west contain the top Crus of St. Émilion. Here, as in St. Émilion, Merlot dominates, and at Château La Gasparde accounts for 85% of the blend. This gives the roundness that typifies clarets from this region; a further 12% Cabernet Franc imparts elegance and 3% Cabernet Sauvignon gives body. The wine is a deep garnet in colour with a nose of plum and berry fruit. The palate is soft yet full with woodland currant and berry fruit with softened tannins giving structure and backbone. There is a lovely long finish making this a superbly soft mature wine.  I am continuing with my reduced selling price of 3 FOR £14! You would only get very basic, young and thin Claret at the supermarket for the price I am asking. Furthermore, wines from neighbouring Châteaux sell for over £11. Is this not the bargain wine of the year?

079       C-D            McGUIGAN – “THE BLACK LABEL” - SE AUSTRALIA – 2002 – 12.5%

The McGuigan family commitment to the Australian wine industry began almost 60 years ago when Perc McGuigan made many of Australia’s greatest wines for Penfolds. Perc passed on the passion and science of winemaking to his eldest son Brian who soon created Wyndham Estate, which rapidly became one of the most famous names in the Australian wine industry. This was due to Brian’s winemaking skills and his wife Fay’s great export and marketing ability. In 1992, after a multi-national conglomerate bought Wyndham Estate, Brian and Fay started over again and McGuigan Wines was born. The Black Label was the flagship range for McGuigan and has continued to be one of the biggest selling Australian wines. This wine, the initial one in the range, is a superbly harmonious blend of Merlot, Shiraz and Grenache grapes from the Murray Valley, which were harvested at the optimum maturity level to produce a beautifully soft style of wine. The colour is garnet red, the palate full of dark stoned and forest fruit flavours with lifted chocolate and cherry aromas. There are cinnamon spicy and peppery notes on the long finish. Quite simply it is gorgeous. Black Label wines are not available in the multiple trade but sell exclusively in the independent sector for up to £5.99; my price is 3 FOR £16.

049       C            ZINFANDEL – “SUTTER HOME – CALIFORNIA – 1998 – 13%

Sutter Home's California Zinfandel is vinified primarily from grapes grown in the Sierra Foothill and Lodi regions, both amongst California's finest sites for the cultivation of California’s signature grape variety. The wine is fermented in stainless steel tanks and then aged for approximately one year in American oak. Sutter Home's 1998 Zinfandel displays a fresh brambleberry aroma with pronounced black pepper spiciness. The bright, medium-bodied, spicy raspberry flavours are rich and sappy, with a zesty varietal spiciness and moderate tannins. Many people only know Zinfandel from the so called “white Zinfandel” blush wine. Ignore that abomination of a wine! Zinfandel produces superb red wines and this is one of California’s best examples in the medium price range. However you won’t have to pay the £6 or so they would ask you on the High Street; my price is 3 FOR £12.
