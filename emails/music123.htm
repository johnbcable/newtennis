Music 1-2-3

For all you music lovers, 2009 vrooms into sight in great shape with events in January, February and March:

Doctor's Orders

To kick off, on 31st January at the clubhouse we have blues band Doctor's Orders returning to the clubhouse.

Tickets are available as always from Peter Morris (01675 442488) and Jenny Roberts (01675 443648) priced at £10 each.

Doctors Orders have played at the clubhouse before and delivered a rollicking set of good-natured blues-rock drawing heavily from traditional blues sources while incorporating covers from artists such as ZZ Top, Wishbone Ash, Allman Brothers and Fleetwood Mac.

Old Play + the Geoff Redfern Disco

On Saturday 28th February we have a fund-raising gig for our new tennis courts in the Scout and Guide Hut from local band Old Play. Before and after gig music is supplied by the Geoff Redfern disco. Tickets are available from Jenny Roberts (01675 443648) priced £12 each. There will be a bar (serving Church End Bitters - lovely) and some half-time nibbles also available on the night. If you've never seen Old Play before then you are in for a treat. They currently feature two "local lads" - Nigel Jones and Glyn Ryland - and perform a good set of covers and floor fillers. A good time will be had by all!

Free At Last 

And in March (Saturday 28th March) the clubhouse plays host to tribute band Free At Last who are a one-stop shop for the music of Free, Bad Company and Paul Rodgers.

Free at Last were formed in 1982 under the name Uncle Sam. Uncle Sam featured three of the current members, Jak (guitar/vocals), Shane (guitar) and Mick (drums) until Dave joined on bass in 1994.
Uncle Sam's set contained a few original songs plus covers by such classic acts as ZZ Top, Pat Travers and of course Free. All the guys in the band have always had a great passion for the music of Free, so in 1996 when deciding to create a tribute there was only one choice who it would be. Since then they have gone from strength to strength headlining at Bill Flynn's Free Convention and playing many of the top venues around the country, universally agreed to be the best Free and Bad Company tribute around. With the original Bad Company reforming to promote their Anthology 2cd set, Paul Rodgers releasing solo albums and joining Queen as their singer for a world tour, it only serves to prove sustained interest in what the band are trying to recreate.
So for a night of 70's style red hot blues influenced, soul stealing ROCK the choice must be FREE AT LAST!

Tickets (priced £10) will be available during February 2009 from the usual sources (Peter Morris 01675 442488 and Jenny Roberts 01675 443648) - first come, first served!
